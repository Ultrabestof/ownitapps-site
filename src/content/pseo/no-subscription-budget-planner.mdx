Most popular budget apps charge every month. YNAB costs $14.99/month. Monarch Money costs $14.99/month. Copilot charges $12.99/month.

For software you use every day to manage your money, that is a significant cost — $100–$180 per year, indefinitely.

There is a better category: **no-subscription budget planners**. Tools you pay for once or use permanently for free.

The subscription model makes sense for software that costs a lot to run — real-time sync servers, bank API connections, support teams. But for a tool that just helps you track your money? A one-time purchase is fair.

### 2. Actual Budget

**Best for:** Zero-based budgeting (YNAB alternative), tech-comfortable users

Actual Budget is a free, open-source budgeting app with optional self-hosted sync. It uses envelope budgeting similar to YNAB but is available for free or as a low-cost hosted option.

**Price:** Free (open-source) or ~$4/month for hosted sync  
**Offline:** Yes (local database)

### 3. GnuCash

**Best for:** Double-entry accounting, detailed financial records

GnuCash is a free, open-source financial management application. It supports double-entry accounting, investment tracking, and detailed reporting.

**Price:** Free  
**Offline:** Yes (desktop app)

### 4. Spreadsheets

**Best for:** Custom setups, full control, simplicity

A well-designed spreadsheet in Excel or Google Sheets Offline is still one of the most flexible budgeting tools available. Free templates exist for monthly budgets, debt tracking, and savings goals.

**Price:** Free to $10/month (if you use Microsoft 365)  
**Offline:** Yes (with downloaded app)

## Who Should Use a No-Subscription Budget Planner?

A no-subscription tool is the right choice if you:
- Want to pay once and stop thinking about it
- Have financial data you'd rather keep off cloud servers
- Are a freelancer who needs tax-ready tracking, not just spending categories
- Travel or work in areas with unreliable internet
- Want software that keeps working even if the company changes pricing

## Who Should Stick With a Subscription App?

Subscription apps may still be the better choice if you:
- Need bank account sync and automatic transaction import
- Want shared access across a household with one subscription
- Need dedicated customer support

## The Bottom Line

The subscription budgeting market is not broken — it is well-designed for users who want connected, automated tracking. But it is the wrong product for users who want control, privacy, and a fixed cost.

For that group, **Solo Finance**, **Actual Budget**, and **GnuCash** are all solid alternatives — each with different strengths and target users.

For freelancers specifically, **Solo Finance** by OwnitApps covers the most relevant needs: income tracking, client management, invoice status, mileage, and tax preparation — all offline, all private, all for a one-time price.
