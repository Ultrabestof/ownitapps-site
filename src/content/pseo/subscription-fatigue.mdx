## What Is Subscription Fatigue?

Subscription fatigue is the mental and financial exhaustion that comes from managing too many recurring software payments. It is not just about the money — it is the cognitive overhead of tracking what you pay, deciding what is worth keeping, and feeling trapped by services that hold your data unless you keep subscribing.

## How Did We Get Here?

Software vendors shifted to subscription models because it is better for their revenue. A user paying $10/month generates more lifetime revenue than one paying $30 once. For companies, subscriptions mean predictable recurring revenue. For users, it means a growing pile of monthly charges.

The shift happened gradually:
- **2010–2015**: Cloud-based tools added subscription tiers
- **2015–2020**: Most new software launched subscription-only
- **2020–2025**: Even one-time purchases converted to subscriptions on renewal
- **2025+**: Subscription stacking became a documented personal finance problem

## The Cloud Lock-In Problem

Subscription fatigue is made worse by cloud lock-in. Your data — financial records, notes, project documents, health records — lives inside the subscription service. When you cancel:

- Access is immediately cut off or limited to 30 days
- Exports may be in proprietary formats
- Some services offer no export at all
- Historical data may be permanently lost

This lock-in is not accidental. It is a retention mechanism designed to make cancellation feel too risky.
