Your budget data is a detailed map of your financial life — income, bills, spending habits, debt levels. Privacy-first budgeting tools treat this data the way it deserves to be treated: stored on your device, invisible to the outside world.

## What "Privacy-First" Actually Means in Budget Tools

Most budget apps are built to aggregate your data. That is often the business model: your spending patterns have value. Privacy-first means the opposite architecture:

- **Data lives locally** — on your machine, in a file or browser storage
- **No account required** — nothing to breach, nothing to sell
- **No sync** — no packets of your financial data moving across networks
- **Works offline** — the tool functions entirely without internet

The best privacy-first budget planners are not stripped-down versions of cloud tools. They are designed from the ground up with local-first storage.

## The Real Risk of Cloud Budget Apps

When Mint shut down in January 2024, millions of users lost access to years of financial data. The data was not returned — it was migrated to Credit Karma's systems. Users had no control.

Beyond shutdowns, cloud finance apps are targets. Breaches expose transaction histories, income figures, and account patterns. That is not a theoretical risk — it has happened repeatedly across the fintech space.

A local-first budget tool eliminates this entire category of risk.
