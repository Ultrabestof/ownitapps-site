## Why Buy-Once Software Is Rare Now

The subscription model became dominant in software because it benefits vendors financially. Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) is predictable, scales with the user base, and encourages product retention through data lock-in.

For buyers, this shift created a fundamentally different relationship with software. Instead of owning tools, users now license access to them — temporarily, conditionally, on the vendor's terms.

## The Case for Owning Your Software

When you buy software once:

- **You own it outright** — the vendor cannot revoke access if you stop paying
- **Your data is yours** — there's no subscription account holding it hostage
- **Long-term costs are lower** — a $20 tool is cheaper than 2 years of a $1/month subscription
- **You're not subsidizing features you don't use** — enterprise collaboration, AI sync, shared workspaces
- **Privacy improves** — fewer reasons to track your usage or monetize your behavior
